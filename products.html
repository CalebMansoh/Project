Oranges are citrus fruits known for their juicy flesh and fragrant, leathery skin. The most common varieties include the sweet orange, sour orange, and mandarin orange. The sweet orange is the most widely cultivated citrus fruit globally.

VEGETABLES

Carrots are highly valued for their nutritional content. They are an excellent source of beta-carotene, which the body converts into vitamin A, essential for maintaining healthy vision and immune function. In a standard serving size of 100 grams, raw carrots provide approximately 41 calories and contain significant amounts of vitamin A (93% of the Daily Value), along with moderate levels of vitamin K and potassium.

Tomato plants are typically vines that can grow up to 3 meters (9.8 feet) tall but are often cultivated as shorter bush varieties. They have hairy stems and leaves that are pinnately compound, with leaflets that can be serrated. The flowers are yellow and bisexual, capable of self-fertilization, which is advantageous for cultivation since traditional pollinators may not always be present.

SEEDS

Maize seeds, commonly known as corn, are a staple food in many parts of the world and are valued for their nutritional content. The composition of maize seeds can vary depending on the variety, but generally, they contain significant amounts of carbohydrates, proteins, fats, vitamins, and minerals.

CROPS

Pepper is one of the well-known and most widely utilized spices all over the world. The crop includes numerous varieties, but the most economically important and widely grown are species from two families, Solanaceae and Piperaceae, which are Capsicum spp. and Piper spp., respectively.

Nuts are a highly nutritious food group that provides a variety of essential nutrients. They are rich in healthy fats, protein, fiber, vitamins, and minerals, nuts are a good source of fibre, healthy fats and plant proteins.
